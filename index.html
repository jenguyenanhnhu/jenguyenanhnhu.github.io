Am I Employable? The Perspective of a History Undergraduate Student in Australia

4 Apr 2021

Footnote Journal | Essay

When you study history, you get constantly asked, "So what job are you going to get?"

Part-memoir, part-history, and part-analysis, I negotiate the unpredictable demands of labour markets and the fluctuating societal value of the humanities and answer the question: am I employable?

Media International Australia | Journal Article

Long before #BlackLivesMatter exploded in 2020, Indigenous Australians have used social media to express and reaffirm their identity, protest racial injustices, and dismantle 'colonial algorithms'.

2021 Ventures Ambassador talks: female entrepreneurs in early modern Vietnam

8 Dec 2021

UQ Ventures | Blog

Did you know that there were female entrepreneurs in early modern Vietnam?

Because yes, they existed. In fact, there were so many female traders that women dominated the marketplaces of Southeast Asia. In this blog, I reflect on the major findings of my Honours research.
